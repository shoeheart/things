://cpbotha.net/2016/07/18/installing-free-lets-encrypt-ssl-certificates-on-webfaction-in-3-easy-steps/

one time (already installed now):
curl https://get.acme.sh | sh

then run:
acme.sh --issue -d codebarn.com -d www.codebarn.com -w ~/webapps/codebarn_com/

it also adds a cron job automatically for:

3 0 * * * "/home/shoeheart/.acme.sh"/acme.sh --cron --home "/home/shoeheart/.acme.sh" > /dev/null

which i changed to to do on 13th ever even month at 4:13am

13 4 13 */2 * "/home/shoeheart/.acme.sh"/acme.sh --cron --home "/home/shoeheart/.acme.sh" > /dev/null

then go to add ssl key and upload the three files (fullchain isn't used):
IGNORE part about supposed to be PEM format.  It accepts these .cer files

[Sun Mar 25 22:51:37 UTC 2018] Your cert is in  /home/shoeheart/.acme.sh/codebarn.com/codebarn.com.cer 
[Sun Mar 25 22:51:37 UTC 2018] Your cert key is in  /home/shoeheart/.acme.sh/codebarn.com/codebarn.com.key 
[Sun Mar 25 22:51:37 UTC 2018] The intermediate CA cert is in  /home/shoeheart/.acme.sh/codebarn.com/ca.cer 

go to website and enable encryption and pick the new codebarn_com cert

finally create 1 more app "www_https_redirect_codebarn_com" with no
content and no ssl and bind to codebarn.com and www.codebarn.com 
with this single .htaccess file:

Options +FollowSymLinks
RewriteEngine on
RewriteCond %{HTTP_HOST} ^www.codebarn.com$ [NC]
RewriteRule ^(.*)$ http://codebarn.com/$1 [R=301,L]

RewriteEngine On
RewriteCond %{HTTP:X-Forwarded-SSL} !on
RewriteRule ^(.*)$ https://%{HTTP_HOST}%{REQUEST_URI} [R=301,L]
